This project came together to articulate the emerging aesthetics of the New York City collectible design scene, which can be at times quite amorphous and hard to define. The intention here was to curate a series of furniture objects that demonstrate a collective vision towards what I perceive as the New York design culture.

## INTERVIEW | In Conversation

> What's the inspiration for this space?

My inspiration for this space was a memory of discovering a library as a young boy. The foggy and blurry line between the fantasy world inside fiction novels and this new third space that was undiscovered for me, felt purely to explore and experience. The inspiration aesthetically was driven primarily by what I found was my take on New York City design culture in 2025. But also with an overlay of my own curative stance and natural aesthetics. I was interested in contrasting stainless steel strong architectural grids with brick that has a soft lumpy clay, warm dark wood grain, mossy greens, blue, and brown. The color palette felt to me natural, but also tech-forward and architecturally rooted in the raw material of wood.

![Wave Exhibit - the room interior with podium and plaster wall](https://formarosacreative.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/07/Practice-NYC-Library-4.jpg "Detail showing debris and magazines on the floor")

![Wave Exhibit - the Wave Column in modular configuration](https://formarosacreative.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/07/Practice-NYC-Library-7.jpg "Side table by Studio Sii, with a biomorphic element")

> Another aspect of this project is the aesthetic intent of Forma Rosa Creative's vision. Forma Rosa Creative is primarily interested in natural aesthetics and natural phenomena. So, how does this project exemplify this directive?

The curation takes on a few objects of natural phenomena. Firstly, a large curtain with a print by Wallpaper Project that depicts a murky green pattern, abstracted from a natural color palette. Of course, a Forma Rosa Creative standing lamp in Botryoidal glory, towering and lurking behind the shelves. A side table by Studio Sii with a strange biomorphic glow and a massive lit rock in the style of Marcus DePaula.

![Wave Exhibit - pendants and standing lamp from the same ceramic module](https://formarosacreative.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/07/Practice-NYC-Library-8.jpg "Diagram showing the curation")

> A. @kikigoti B. @formarosastudio C. @knownwork D. @studio.s.ii E. @njroseti F. @wallpaperprojects G. @marcusv_depaula

The architecture itself was also carefully curated in natural materials: dark-stained wood with a strong structural grid making up the bookshelves and columns. As you can see in the image, the space is also double-height with a large open ceiling, letting ample light in while also giving a sense of intimacy. The floor is covered by brick, which I often use because it feels almost like the street is entering the interior. And I find that the relationship between the outside and the inside should be blurred and broken. Sometimes, I look to challenge that threshold of the entrance to an interior space.

![Wave Exhibit - Wave Column configuration study](https://formarosacreative.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/07/Practice-NYC-Library-2.jpg "Bookshelf detail and sconce")

## Library Typology

Forma Rosa Creative took on the library as a typology with the explicit intent of creating a moody, dark, yet eclectic and lounge-like feel. The library is an interesting typology because it is a gathering space. A space where you want to sit and rest, introspect, and perceive the goals of the typology itself; A library is to contain information. It is to expand the mind. It is to generate new experiences.

![Wave Exhibit - tadelakt benches and a scattering of spotlight minis at dusk](https://formarosacreative.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/07/Practice-NYC-Library-3.jpg "Strange lamp lurking in the coridors")

So, in this sense, I decided to go back into my memories and find a place of a young child exploring a library for the first time. I envisioned myself exploring the depths of novels, fiction, fairy tales, and knowledge of the past. I envision myself peering and walking through long corridors, looking for the new and novel. The lighting in the space, of course, was warm and moody, primarily using wall sconces and low lamps. In contrast, the large rock light gives a natural 5,000K light.
