Off a Soho street, the ground turns to pasture — green underfoot, a horizon at eye level.

Wave Exhibit by Forma Rosa Creative is an immersive environment built around the Wave Column, and a nod to the picturesque at gallery scale — an antithesis to technological reform, a return to our pastoral heritage. Meditative and gritty nature, real dirt pulling you in with all senses, ceramic sculptures that read as both artifact and organism, glowing as if alive.

A continuous podium wraps the room's perimeter, and the backing undulates in an echo to the column's own wave-form. The hand-applied plaster, pigmented from deep green through beige into dark blue, runs bottom to top — pasture, dry earth, dusk sky — centering your view to the horizon.

Mirrored walls kaleidoscope, expanding the view into what Jay Appleton calls prospect: an unbroken sightline deep into the distance.

Three Wave Columns in various modular configurations anchor the gallery, while three more pull visitors in from the window display. Pendants and a standing lamp use the same ceramic module and showcase the different functions. Real dirt mixed with fall leaves is held in a plexiglass case at the window as if the earth is being cut through. Tadelakt is hand-applied to plywood, all the corners sanded smooth, giving a rich and full texture for the window display set and benches. A scattering of spotlight minis brings another dimension to the evening window display: a memory of stars at dusk over a campsite in the San Juan Islands, carried indoors.
