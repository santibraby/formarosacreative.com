Concept NYC Library

Environments composed
as landscapes.

Every space starts with the user experience - how you move, what you smell, how your depth of view expands. Deep sightlines let your nervous system settle. Real material - dirt, clay, pigmented plaster - convinces the senses the room is alive.

The atelier is led by Santiago Braby Brown - architect by training (B.Arch, Pratt Institute), creative director by practice. His 10+ years span healthcare architecture, computational facade engineering, and high-end residential interiors. He co-founded Forma Rosa, the ceramic lighting studio shown at Milan Design Week and covered by The New York Times and Elle Decor. Projects are developed through advanced 3D modeling and computational solutions, prototyped by hand, and built with fabricators assembled per project - craft and technology, the method in service of something older.

We take on a small number of projects each year, and give each the studio's full attention - concept through installation.
