Off a Soho street, the ground turns to pasture — green underfoot, a horizon at eye level.

01 — CONCEPT

Wave Exhibit is an immersive environment designed to host the Wave Column in its many variations, a ceramic fixture by Forma Rosa Studio.

The spatial concept is a nod to the picturesque movement at gallery scale — an antithesis to the contemporary technological reform, and a return to our pastoral heritage. Meditative and gritty, both beautiful yet sublime: real dirt installed in the window display pulls you in with an added sense, and ceramic sculptures that read as both artifact and organism stand tall like tree trunks and glow as if alive.

02 — THE ROOM

A continuous podium wraps the room's perimeter, and the backing undulates in a wave form echoing the column's own feminine silhouette. The hand-applied tadelakt, pigmented from deep green into sandy beige then into dark blue, runs from the ground all the way up, covering the podiums, walls and ceilings — signaling pasture, dry earth, dusk sky, and centering your view to the horizon.

Mirrored walls kaleidoscope, expanding your view into what Jay Appleton calls prospect: an unbroken sightline deep into the distance.

03 — THE COLUMNS

Three Wave Columns in various modular configurations anchor the gallery, while three more pull visitors in from the window display. Pendants and a standing lamp showcase the different functions of the collection, built around a ceramic module.

04 — THE WINDOW

Real dirt mixed with fall leaves — the scent of a walk in the park in the fall — is held in a plexiglass case at the window, as if the landscape has been sliced, and we can see the layers just beneath our feet. Tadelakt is hand-applied to plywood, all the corners sanded smooth, giving a rich and full texture for the window display set and interior scenography.

A scattering of spotlight minis brings another dimension to the evening window display: a memory of stars at dusk over a campsite in the San Juan Islands, carried indoors.

The space acts as a portal — I want visitors to feel as if they are remembering a moment from their childhood: nostalgia, but also alienation, as you might feel walking a new trail by yourself in the late fall.
