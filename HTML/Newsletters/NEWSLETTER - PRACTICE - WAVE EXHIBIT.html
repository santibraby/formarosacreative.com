Off a Soho street, the ground turns to pasture — green underfoot, a horizon at eye level.

01 — Concept

Wave Exhibit is an immersive environment designed to host the Wave Column in its many variations, a ceramic fixture by Forma Rosa Studio.

The spatial concept is a nod to the picturesque movement at gallery scale — a return to our pastoral heritage. Meditative and gritty: real dirt in the window display pulls you in with an added sense, and ceramic sculptures that read as both artifact and organism stand tall like tree trunks and glow as if alive.

02 — The Room

A continuous podium wraps the room's perimeter, and the backing undulates in a wave form echoing the column's own feminine silhouette. Hand-applied tadelakt, pigmented from deep green into sandy beige then into dark blue, runs from the ground all the way up — signaling pasture, dry earth, dusk sky, and centering your view to the horizon.

Mirrored walls kaleidoscope, expanding your view into an unbroken sightline deep into the distance.

03 — The Window

Real dirt mixed with fall leaves — the scent of a walk in the park in the fall — is held in a plexiglass case at the window, as if the landscape has been sliced and we can see the layers just beneath our feet. A scattering of spotlight minis brings another dimension to the evening display: a memory of stars at dusk over a campsite in the San Juan Islands, carried indoors.

 

The space acts as a portal — I want visitors to feel as if they are remembering a moment from their childhood: nostalgia, but also alienation, as you might feel walking a new trail by yourself in the late fall.
